On January 7, 2026, Renee Good was murdered as she was swerving the car away from an ICE agent. The video of this extrajudicial execution is widely available online, from multiple angles. I will not belabor this point: Good was driving away from the agent, and the interactions beforehand indicated no threat towards the agent. This is observable reality to anyone who has watched the video.

The evening of the shooting, DHS Secretary and known dog-killer Kristi Noem went on national television and said Good “attempted to run a law enforcement officer over”, calling it “an act of domestic terrorism.” Couchfucker and vice-president JD Vance joined the fray the next day, suggesting that Good was determined to kill a law enforcement officer, and that it was “classic terrorism”.[^1]

This sort of rejection of reality is not new for conservatives, and for the Trump campaign in specific. When pressed, JD Vance admitted that his oft-echoed claim about Haitian migrants eating pets in Springfield was, in fact, entirely fictitious. "If I have to create stories so that the American media actually pays attention to the suffering of the American people, then that’s what I’m going to do," he tells CNN.[^2]

This is not an attempt at spreading misinformation. It is not misinformation when you patently and objectively contradict a video that anyone can watch. You do not go on national television and admit you made up stories as a form of misinformation. What we are witnessing, in its full glory, is mythmaking.[^3]

It does not matter whether a statement reflects the facts of a matter. The facts, indeed, are largely irrelevant to whether it is true or not. What defines truth is instead how it corresponds to <span class="myth">the Myth</span>. A righteous officer of the law acting in self defense against a violent agitator. A nationwide crackdown on the scourge of immigrants, deporting all those who have sullied the sanctity of this land. <span class="myth">The Myth</span> of an America that was once great.

This is, of course, just one manifestation of <span class="myth">the Myth</span>. <span class="myth">The Myth</span> is syncretic and comes in many forms, expedient to the specific proto-fascist movement in question. Common themes include a glorious past we must return to, odes toward a natural hierarchical social structure, and the presence of virulent bigotry of all kinds. However, the defining aspect of <span class="myth">the Myth</span> is that it supersedes reality, bends it to its will. Truth is dictated by <span class="myth">the Myth</span>. Facts and evidence against the truth is utterly irrelevant, because you already know the truth.

In this framework, a fascist can say something that is entirely false, that they know to be false, and yet still not be lying. Because they’re telling the truth; the truth that corresponds to <span class="myth">the Myth</span>. To debate a fascist on the basis of facts and evidence is a fool’s errand.[^4] Sartre said it best:

> “Never believe that anti-Semites are completely unaware of the absurdity of their replies. They know that their remarks are frivolous, and open to challenge. But they are amusing themselves, for it is their adversary who is obliged to use words responsibly since he believes in words. (…) If then (…), the anti-Semite is impervious to reason and to experience, it is not because his conviction is strong. Rather his conviction is strong because he has chosen first of all to be impervious.”[^5]

How does one combat <span class="myth">the Myth</span>? Pull it by its roots, or supersede it with your own. The specific mechanism I leave as an exercise to the reader, but I will say this: [a thrown brick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Cable_Street) does not discriminate.
